About Me!

I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Maryland, College Park, graduating in 2023. While in undergrad, I helped develop class materials for ASTR310 (Observational Astronomy) and then became a teacher's assistant for ASTR310 and ASTR121 (Introductory Astrophysics II). I earned the 2023 Outstanding Undergraduate Teaching Award from the UMD Astronomy Department. I was also involved in research with Dr. Sylvain Veilleux, analyzing UV scattering in the M82 cone.

I am now a postbaccalaureate researcher at UMD/NASA Goddard, working with PRIME Telescope on the development of an image processing and photometric pipeline. This pipeline, though still in active development, has allowed the UMD/Goddard PRIME team to post numerous GCN notices.

Unrelated to astronomy, I also do amateur photography! I've been hoarding my photos for months but have finally started to share some here!

Prime-focus Infrared Microlensing Experiment (PRIME) Telescope

PRIME is part of the joint NASA-JAXA collaboration providing support to the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope. It conducts on-sky NIR observations through microlensing surveys in the Galactic bulge along with GRB and GW studies.
